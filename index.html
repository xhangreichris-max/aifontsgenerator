What Is a Glitch Text Generator?

Glitch text is text that looks broken on purpose. It uses Unicode's combining characters — small diacritics that normally sit above or below a letter — to stack so many marks on a single character that it overflows, creating that corrupted, unstable look you see on gaming profiles and horror-themed social accounts.

The most well-known version of this is zalgo text. Zalgo stacks diacritics aggressively: the characters bleed upward and downward, sometimes consuming the lines of text around them. Lighter glitch styles use strikethrough marks or scattered corrupted characters to give a subtler distortion effect. All of it is just Unicode — which means it pastes and displays correctly on any platform that supports Unicode text, which is basically everything.

Gamers were among the first to run hard with glitch text for in-game names and clan tags. It reads as "corrupted" or "hacked," which fits the competitive and horror-game aesthetic. Social media users picked it up for bios and usernames that needed to stand out. The glitch text generator at AI Fonts Generator handles the whole spectrum, from light strikethrough distortion to full zalgo overflow, so you can dial in how corrupted you actually want your text to look.

80+ Font Styles — What's Inside

Classic Styles

Classic styles are the most universally compatible output in the generator. This category covers serif Unicode, gothic blackletter, bold Unicode, italic Unicode, bold italic, and several variations of each. When you type a word in bold Unicode, every character gets replaced by its Unicode bold equivalent. It looks visually identical to bolded text, but it pastes as plain text that any app can display.

Gothic or blackletter fonts give text a medieval, heavy look without being unreadable. They work well for gaming names, music-related profiles, and anyone running a darker aesthetic. Serif styles add a formal, editorial feel and tend to display cleanly across the widest range of platforms. If you want something that looks noticeably different from default text without getting too experimental, classic styles are the right category to start.

Glitch & Corrupted Text

This is where things get weird in a good way. The glitch and corrupted text category includes true zalgo output, medium and light glitch styles, strikethrough text, and combinations of these. Zalgo stacks diacritics above and below letters until the text visually falls apart. The effect is intentional and striking. For gaming usernames, horror accounts, and anything meant to feel unstable or transgressive, this category is the first place to look.

Strikethrough text is a milder option in the same family. The characters are legible but carry a struck-out visual effect, which reads as sardonic or self-aware on social media. The glitch text generator outputs multiple intensity levels, so you can go full zalgo overflow or keep it to light character corruption depending on what the platform supports.

Exotic & International Scripts

These styles replace standard Latin characters with Unicode characters that resemble them but come from other writing systems or symbol sets. Japanese-style output maps Latin letters to katakana equivalents or full-width characters, giving text a Japanese aesthetic without being actual Japanese. Runic styles use Elder Futhark or stylized rune-like characters that feel old and cryptic. Symbol-mapped styles use geometric shapes and mathematical symbols to reconstruct readable text from non-letter characters.

The legibility varies across this category. Some styles are immediately readable at a glance. Others take a second. That second-look quality is part of the appeal for usernames and display names where you want people to pause and look twice.

Remix & Fusion Styles

Remix and fusion styles wrap decorative characters around the text itself. This might mean small symbols between each letter, bracket-like characters framing the whole word, or decorative marks that turn "Alex" into something that reads like a guild tag from a fantasy game. The effect is less about replacing letters and more about surrounding them with visual decoration.

These styles tend to display well in Discord bios and guild names, Instagram display names, and mobile game usernames where you want a styled name rather than a corrupted or script-style one. This is also the category where the tool feels closest to a fancy text generator for someone who wants decorative text without distortion.

What is the difference between glitch text and zalgo text?

Zalgo is a specific type of glitch text. Glitch text is a broader category that includes zalgo but also covers lighter distortion styles, strikethrough effects, and mixed corrupted characters that do not overflow the line. Zalgo is the most extreme version of glitch text.
